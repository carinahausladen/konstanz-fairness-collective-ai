### Part II · Defining & Measuring Bias — [Weeks 2–3](03.md)

The social-science machinery for measuring bias, and what changes when the subject is an LLM. Three sub-blocks:

- **Bias metrics.** Allport's stereotype/prejudice/discrimination split, the Stereotype Content Model (warmth × competence), the IAT (implicit vs. explicit), taste-based vs. statistical discrimination (Becker; Arrow–Phelps), the audit-study design (Bertrand–Mullainathan), intersectionality (Crenshaw) and Simpson's paradox.
- **Datasets for bias evaluation.** Gallegos's benchmark taxonomy (counterfactual inputs vs. prompts). Papers: `Bailey 2022` (*people = men*, embedding geometry), `Bai 2025` (explicitly unbiased LLMs still form biased associations), `An 2025` (intersectional resume audit).
- **Benchmarks vs. agentic evaluation.** Bias as an *output property* vs. *behaviour along a trajectory*. Paper: `Lynch 2025` (agentic misalignment), read against principal–agent theory and the red-teaming tradition.

### Part III · Social Choice for AI — [Weeks 4–5](04.md)

Alignment reframed as a "many → one" aggregation problem.

- **In principle ([Week 4](04.md)).** Gabriel's value-ladder; Arrow's impossibility; the reward model as a Bradley–Terry voting rule. Papers: `Gabriel 2020`, `Conitzer 2024`; datasets `Kirk 2024` (PRISM) and `Zhang 2026` (Community Alignment), which expose preference heterogeneity.
- **In deployment ([Week 5](05.md)).** The aggregation rule meets a live platform. Papers: `Donahue & Raghavan 2026` (leaderboards as voting rules), `Chuai 2026` (bridging-based Community Notes), `Nudo 2026` (the hyperactive minority).

### Part IV · Economic Impacts of AI — [Week 6](06.md)

Economic impact treated as a fairness question, where the choice of measurement frame decides whose harm becomes visible. `Acemoglu 2021` (mechanisms of harm), `Teutloff 2025` (freelance-market displacement), `Massenkoff 2026` (observed exposure), `Ranjit 2026` (the meaning of work).

### Part V · Democracy & LLMs — [Week 7](07.md)

Where LLMs enter democratic processes, along a *replace ↔ assist* axis. `Gudiño 2024` (the digital-twin voter — replace), `Tessler 2024` (the Habermas Machine — assist), `Kurtulus 2025` (political bias as an audit target).
