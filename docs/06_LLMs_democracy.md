# Democracy and Large Language Models  
**Short Lecture Notes – Week 03**

## 1. LLMs as potential replacements / proxies for humans

**Large Language Models (LLMs) as Agents for Augmented Democracy** (2024)  
Jairo F. Gudiño-Rosero, Umberto Grandi, César A. Hidalgo  

- Fine-tuned LLMs become “digital twins” that predict voter preferences  
- Tested on Brazil 2022 election data (67 policies)  
- Can augment / stand in for citizens in large-scale voting  
- Outperform simple ideological heuristics at population level  
- Raises legitimacy concerns if humans are fully replaced  

**Citation**  
Gudiño-Rosero, J. F., Grandi, U., & Hidalgo, C. A. (2024). Large Language Models (LLMs) as Agents for Augmented Democracy. *arXiv:2405.03452*. Also in *Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society A*, 382(2285), 20240100.

## 2. LLMs struggle to represent human diversity

**LLM Voting: Human Choices and AI Collective Decision Making** (2024, updated)  
Joshua C. Yang, Damian Dailisan, Marcin Korecki, Carina I. Hausladen, Dirk Helbing  

- LLMs (GPT-4, LLaMA-2 etc.) produce more homogeneous voting patterns  
- Less diversity than real human groups → training data bias  
- Threat to pluralism and minority representation in simulations  

**Citation**  
Yang, J. C., Dailisan, D., Korecki, M., Hausladen, C. I., & Helbing, D. (2024). LLM Voting: Human Choices and AI Collective Decision Making. *arXiv:2402.01766v3*.

**Reasoning Boosts Opinion Alignment in LLMs** (2026)  
Frédéric Berdoz, Yann Billeter, Yann Vonlanthen, Roger Wattenhofer (ETH Zurich)  

- Chain-of-thought + RL reasoning improves alignment with human opinions  
- Tested on U.S., European, Swiss political datasets  
- Still persistent biases → not yet faithful political digital twins  

**Citation**  
Berdoz, F., Billeter, Y., Vonlanthen, Y., & Wattenhofer, R. (2026). Reasoning Boosts Opinion Alignment in LLMs. *arXiv:2603.01214*. (Accepted at ICLR 2026)

## 3. Participation = human well-being & dignity  
John Tasioulas

- Democratic participation is intrinsically valuable  
- Essential for autonomy, dignity, and human flourishing  
- AI must **support** genuine human agency — not displace it  
- Risk: reducing people to passive data inputs harms well-being  

**Key references**  
- Tasioulas, J. (2022). Artificial Intelligence, Humanistic Ethics. *Daedalus*, 151(2), 232–243.  
- Tasioulas, J. (2025). “Artificial Intelligence and Democracy: From Threat to Promise?” (EUI lecture)

## 4. Supporting participation (instead of replacement)

**Recommender systems for democratic participation**

- Suggest relevant policies, diverse viewpoints, forums, stakeholders  
- Increase informed engagement and inclusion  
- Examples: civic/news recommenders, participatory deliberation tools  
- Goal: enhance human agency and well-being (aligns with Tasioulas)  
- Challenge: avoid bias in recommendations → needs transparency & co-design  

**Selected references**  
- Helberger, N. (2019). On the Democratic Role of News Recommenders. *Digital Journalism*, 7(8), 993–1012.  
- Bojic, L. et al. (2025). Towards Recommender Systems LLMs Playground (RecSysLLMsP). *arXiv:2502.00055*

**Core takeaway**  
LLMs enable powerful augmentation but risk homogenization and loss of agency.  
Prioritize **human-centered support** tools (e.g., recommenders) that preserve dignity and meaningful participation.
