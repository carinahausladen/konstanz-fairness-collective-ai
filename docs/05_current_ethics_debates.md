# The Department of Defense Controversy

- Anthropic had cooperated with the Department of Defense for a long time. They could not agree on two issues: fully autonomous weapons and mass domestic surveillance.
- The Department of Defense ended its cooperation with Anthropic.
- Sam Altman first praised Anthropic’s decision. Later, Altman announced that OpenAI would work with the Department of Defense.
- This caused a big outcry in the AI community and sparked the “QuitGPT” / #BoycottGPT movement.
- One critic said the QuitGPT movement turns a political crisis into a lifestyle choice.

# Privacy and AI

- Major AI companies collect and train on user data by default, often with poor disclosure.
- This includes both chatbots (like GPT) and always-on hardware like Meta smart glasses.
- Smart glasses + agentic systems (e.g. VisionClaw with Gemini) now combine real-time vision, voice, and action.
- Thinking “this is only an American issue thanks to GDPR” is wrong — Proton Mail founder recently warned that European protections are insufficient against always-on AI.

# Infrastructure & Resource Use

- America’s top electricity think tank (EPRI) recently raised its data center electricity forecast by 60%.
- The energy demands of large-scale AI systems are under growing scrutiny as data centers consume an increasing share of national electricity.
- Sam Altman defended AI’s high energy use by comparing it to humans: “It also takes a lot of energy to train a human.”
- AI creates secondary costs too. Wikipedia, for example, now blocks or restricts bots that scrape its content for training.
- Yet Big Tech continues to claim that Generative AI will help “save the planet” — a position many critics call greenwashing due to limited evidence.

# Vibe Research and its Consequences

- AI-generated “slop” is flooding repositories with low-quality papers.
- arXiv will become independent from Cornell on July 1, 2026, and is hiring a CEO to handle exploding submissions and AI-generated content.
- Common failure: Models like Claude write conclusions before data analysis (“vibe research”).
- Researchers urge a pause: we must rethink responsible use of AI in science.
- Study (Klingbeil et al.): People dangerously over-rely on AI advice — even when it hurts them and others — simply because they know it is AI-generated.
- Warning: Science may be nearing a tipping point due to this flood of low-value content.
