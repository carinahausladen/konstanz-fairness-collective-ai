# The Economics of AI Fairness

AI reshapes the distribution of wealth, opportunity, and power.

---

## 1. Unequal Distribution of Benefits

- AI delivers the largest productivity gains for higher-skilled workers and complex, knowledge-intensive tasks  
  ([Anthropic Economic Index Report: Economic Primitives](https://www.anthropic.com/research/economic-index-economic-primitives))

- Example: ~12× speedups for advanced tasks  
  ([Estimating AI productivity gains from Claude conversations](https://www.anthropic.com/research/estimating-ai-productivity-gains))

---

## 2. Labor Market Effects
[Labor market impacts of AI: A new measure and early evidence](https://www.anthropic.com/research/labor-market-impacts)
[Productivity Gains and Labor Pains: What Will AI Do to Jobs?](https://www.hoover.org/events/productivity-gains-and-labor-pains-what-will-ai-do-jobs)
[Visualization](https://www.visualcapitalist.com/mapped-which-countries-use-claude-ai-the-most/)
[X comment](https://x.com/cryptopunk7213/status/2029699441226129479?s=20)

- Many jobs show high AI task exposure (e.g., up to 75%)  
- No clear rise in unemployment (yet)  
- Early signals: reduced entry-level hiring in exposed fields  

---

## 3. Global Inequality

- AI adoption is uneven across countries  
  ([Anthropic Economic Index Report: Uneven geographic and enterprise AI adoption](https://www.anthropic.com/research/economic-index-geography))

- Usage often concentrated in high-income regions and tech sectors  
  ([India Country Brief: The Anthropic Economic Index](https://www.anthropic.com/research/india-economic-index))
