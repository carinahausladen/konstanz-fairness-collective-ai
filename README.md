# Fairness and Collective Decision-Making in AI
**Summer Semester 2026 · University of Konstanz**


This seminar introduces analytical methods for studying fairness, bias, and participation in AI from a social science perspective. 
The course focuses on understanding, evaluating, and interpreting their societal and political implications. 
It builds on political theory, social choice, and fairness concepts to analyze AI methods critically, emphasizing qualitative reasoning alongside computational evaluation.

## Topics 
### Block 1 · Bias and Fairness in Large Language Models
**Concepts:** types of social bias and harm; fairness notions in NLP and political contexts  
**Metrics:** embedding-based; probability-based; generation-based  
**Data:** bias benchmarks; validity, reliability, and coverage challenges  

### Block 2 · Social Choice and LLM Alignment
Preference formation and aggregation · value pluralism · limits of fairness guarantees  

### Block 3 · AI in Deliberative Democracy and Ethics
**Roles:** moderator/facilitator · accessibility (speech-to-text, translation) · persona simulation  
**Evaluation:** participation · legitimacy · contestability · power effects  

---

## Learning Goals

- Understand core concepts of **bias, fairness, and alignment** in large language models.
- Apply and interpret **methods for measuring bias and fairness**, including metrics and datasets.
- Analyze **AI-supported deliberation** with respect to participation, legitimacy, and democratic values.
- Critically connect **technical evaluation results** to their social and political implications.

---

## Course Evaluation and Format

- Hands-on analysis of models, metrics, and datasets  
- Student presentations and written papers
