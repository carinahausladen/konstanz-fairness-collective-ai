- Explicitly unbiased large language models still form biased associations, `Bai 2025`
- Measuring gender and racial biases in large language models: Intersectional evidence from automated resume evaluation, `An 2025`
- Agentic Misalignment: How LLMs Could Be Insider Threats, `Lynch 2025`

**Social Choice for AI**
- Artificial Intelligence, Values, and Alignment, `Gabriel 2020`
- Social Choice Should Guide AI Alignment in Dealing with Diverse Human Feedback, `Conitzer 2024`
- The PRISM Alignment Dataset, `Kirk 2024`
- The Community Alignment Dataset, `Zhang 2026`
- Impacts of Aggregation on Model Diversity and Consumer Utility, `Donahue 2026`
- Consensus Stability of Community Notes on X, `Chuai 2026`
- Hyperactive Minority Alters the Stability of Community Notes, `Nudo 2026`

**Economic Impacts of AI**
- Harms of AI, `Acemoglu 2021`
- Winners and Losers of Generative AI, `Teutloff 2025`
- Labor Market Impacts of AI: A New Measure and Early Evidence, `Massenkoff 2026`
- Are We Automating the Joy Out of Work? Designing AI to Augment Work, Not Meaning, `Ranjit 2026`

**Democracy and LLMs**
- Large Language Models (LLMs) as Agents for Augmented Democracy, `Gudiño 2024`
- AI can help humans find common ground in democratic deliberation, `Tessler 2024`
- Political Bias in Large Language Models: A Case Study on the 2025 German Federal Election, `Kurtulus 2025`

## Activities

### 1. Reading Notes (ungraded)

For each reading, you should briefly (1 sentence each, via [![Overleaf](https://img.shields.io/badge/Overleaf-Project-47A141?logo=overleaf&logoColor=white)](https://www.overleaf.com/7678674488hfmsgbmsyszc#8f42d3)):

- What is one specific idea or result you found interesting, and why?
- What is one point that remains unclear? What specific question would you ask in class?
- How could this paper be extended? For example, could its methods be applied in a different context? Can its data be analyzed in a different way?

### 2. Discussant Role (graded, 30%)

- You will serve once (or twice, depending on class size) as a discussant for your chosen reading(s).
- Your role is to help the class understand the paper in depth and to generate ideas for replication or extension.
- You will give a short (10–15 min) presentation and lead a brief (5–8 min) discussion.


### 3. Research Project (graded, 70%)

Your goal is to develop an original research paper that builds on the course literature.
You may work individually or in small groups.  

Only final paper (~8 pages / 4,000–8,000 words) will be graded. In addition, submitting the following milestones is mandatory:

- **May 27:** Abstract of your project idea
- **Jun 10:** Draft the introduction and literature gap  
- **Jun 17:** Present your initial results to the class; set up a GitHub repo
- **Jun 24:** Submit a first full draft  
- **Jul 08:** Prepare slides, a practice presentation, and a social media-style summary
- **Jul 15:** Final presentation
- **Aug 15:** Submit final paper and GitHub repo

All milestones must be submitted as GitHub Issues.
Title format: Week X – [Your Name]
